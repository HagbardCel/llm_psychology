# Freudian Psychoanalysis

## Core Concepts

### The Unconscious Mind
Freud proposed that the mind has three levels:
- **Conscious**: What we are aware of at any given moment
- **Preconscious**: What we can easily bring to consciousness
- **Unconscious**: Thoughts, memories, and desires that are repressed but still influence behavior

### The Structure of Personality
Freud's structural model divides the psyche into three parts:
- **Id**: The primitive, instinctual part that operates on the pleasure principle
- **Ego**: The rational part that mediates between the id and reality
- **Superego**: The moral component that incorporates societal rules and values

### Psychosexual Development
Freud believed personality develops through five stages:
1. **Oral Stage** (0-18 months): Focus on mouth and oral activities
2. **Anal Stage** (18 months-3 years): Focus on bowel and bladder control
3. **Phallic Stage** (3-6 years): Focus on genitalia and Oedipus/Electra complexes
4. **Latency Stage** (6-puberty): Sexual feelings are dormant
5. **Genital Stage** (puberty onward): Mature sexual interests

### Defense Mechanisms
Unconscious psychological strategies used to cope with anxiety:
- **Repression**: Pushing distressing thoughts into the unconscious
- **Denial**: Refusing to accept reality
- **Projection**: Attributing one's own unacceptable thoughts to others
- **Sublimation**: Channeling unacceptable impulses into socially acceptable activities
- **Displacement**: Redirecting emotions from the original source to a safer target

## Therapeutic Techniques

### Free Association
Patients are encouraged to speak freely about whatever comes to mind, allowing unconscious thoughts to surface.

### Dream Analysis
Freud called dreams the "royal road to the unconscious." He distinguished between:
- **Manifest content**: The actual dream imagery
- **Latent content**: The hidden, symbolic meaning

### Transference and Countertransference
- **Transference**: Patient's unconscious feelings toward the analyst (often parental figures)
- **Countertransference**: Analyst's emotional reactions to the patient

## Key Therapeutic Goals

- Bringing unconscious material into consciousness
- Resolving internal conflicts between the id, ego, and superego
- Understanding the influence of early childhood experiences
- Achieving insight into the root causes of psychological distress
