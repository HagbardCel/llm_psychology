# Carl Jung - Key Concepts

## The Unconscious
Jung agreed with Freud's concept of the unconscious but expanded it significantly.
*   **Personal Unconscious:** Similar to Freud's unconscious, containing repressed or forgotten personal experiences.
*   **Collective Unconscious:** A deeper layer shared by all humanity, containing universal memories and ideas (archetypes) inherited from our ancestors.

## The Psyche's Structure
Jung moved away from Freud's structural model (Id, Ego, Superego).
*   **Ego:** The center of consciousness, our sense of self and identity.
*   **Shadow:** The unconscious aspect of the personality which the conscious ego doesn't identify with. It contains repressed weaknesses, shortcomings, and instincts. Integrating the shadow is crucial for personal growth.
*   **Anima/Animus:**
    *   **Anima:** The feminine inner personality present in men.
    *   **Animus:** The masculine inner personality present in women.
    These represent the contrasexual aspects and are essential for achieving wholeness.
*   **Self:** The unified consciousness and unconsciousness of a person. The goal of individuation is to realize the Self.

## Archetypes
Universal symbols or themes found within the collective unconscious. They are not fixed but are potential forms that influence how we experience and respond to the world.
*   **The Shadow:** The dark, hidden side of the personality.
*   **The Anima/Animus:** The contrasexual aspect.
*   **The Persona:** The "mask" we wear in public, the social face we present to the world.
*   **The Self:** The unified, whole personality (the goal of development).
*   **The Great Mother:** Represents fertility, nourishment, and destruction.
*   **The Wise Old Man:** Represents wisdom and meaning.
*   **The Hero:** Represents the ego's journey to achieve wholeness.
*   **The Child:** Represents innocence, potential, and rebirth.

## Psychological Types
Jung introduced the concept of psychological types, which later influenced the development of the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI).
*   **Attitudes:**
    *   **Extraversion (E):** Energy is directed outward, towards the external world of people and things.
    *   **Introversion (I):** Energy is directed inward, towards the internal world of thoughts and ideas.
*   **Functions:**
    *   **Sensation (S):** Focuses on concrete, immediate reality.
    *   **Intuition (N):** Focuses on possibilities, patterns, and future implications.
    *   **Thinking (T):** Makes decisions based on logic and objective principles.
    *   **Feeling (F):** Makes decisions based on personal values and subjective considerations.

## Individuation
The central goal of Jungian psychology. It is the process of integrating the conscious and unconscious parts of the psyche to achieve a balanced, whole individual (the Self). It involves:
1.  Becoming aware of the unconscious (through dreams, imagination, etc.).
2.  Confronting and integrating the Shadow.
3.  Developing the Anima/Animus.
4.  Ultimately, achieving a sense of unity and wholeness.
