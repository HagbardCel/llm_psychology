# Sigmund Freud - Key Concepts

## The Unconscious
Freud proposed that the mind has three levels of awareness:
1.  **Conscious:** Thoughts we are currently aware of.
2.  **Preconscious:** Memories and thoughts that can easily be brought to consciousness.
3.  **Unconscious:** The largest part, containing repressed feelings, thoughts, urges, and memories. This is the source of much of our behavior.

## The Structure of Personality (The Psychic Apparatus)
Freud believed personality has three components:
1.  **Id:** The primitive, instinctual part. Operates on the pleasure principle, seeking immediate gratification of desires (e.g., hunger, sex, aggression).
2.  **Ego:** The rational, decision-making part. Operates on the reality principle, mediating between the id's desires and the constraints of reality and the superego.
3.  **Superego:** The moral component. Incorporates societal rules and parental guidance, striving for perfection and imposing guilt or pride.

## Psychosexual Development Stages
Freud theorized that personality develops through a series of childhood stages, each focused on a different erogenous zone:
1.  **Oral Stage (0-1 year):** Pleasure centers on the mouth. Fixation can lead to issues like overeating or smoking in adulthood.
2.  **Anal Stage (1-3 years):** Focus on bowel and bladder control. Toilet training is crucial. Fixation can result in anal-retentive (excessively orderly) or anal-expulsive (messy, disorganized) personalities.
3.  **Phallic Stage (3-6 years):** Focus on the genitals. Children develop unconscious sexual desires for the opposite-sex parent (Oedipus complex for boys, Electra complex for girls). Resolution involves identifying with the same-sex parent.
4.  **Latency Stage (6-puberty):** Sexual feelings are dormant. Children focus on same-sex friendships and skill development.
5.  **Genital Stage (puberty onward):** Mature sexual interests emerge. The goal is to form healthy romantic relationships.

## Defense Mechanisms
The ego uses these unconscious strategies to reduce anxiety arising from conflicts between the id and superego or from external stressors:
*   **Repression:** Pushing distressing thoughts into the unconscious.
*   **Denial:** Refusing to accept reality or facts.
*   **Projection:** Attributing one's own unacceptable thoughts or feelings to someone else.
*   **Displacement:** Redirecting emotions from the original source to a safer target.
*   **Sublimation:** Channeling unacceptable impulses into socially acceptable activities (e.g., turning aggression into sports).
*   **Rationalization:** Creating false but plausible excuses to explain behavior.
*   **Regression:** Reverting to an earlier stage of development in times of stress.
*   **Reaction Formation:** Converting unconscious wishes or impulses into their opposites.
