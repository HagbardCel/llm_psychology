# Jungian Analytical Psychology

## Core Concepts

### The Unconscious Mind
Jung expanded Freud's concept of the unconscious:
- **Personal Unconscious**: Repressed or forgotten personal experiences
- **Collective Unconscious**: Universal, inherited psychological patterns shared by all humanity

### Archetypes
Universal symbols and themes found in the collective unconscious:
- **The Shadow**: The repressed, denied, or undeveloped aspects of the personality
- **The Anima/Animus**: The feminine aspect in men / masculine aspect in women
- **The Self**: The unified, whole personality; the goal of individuation
- **The Persona**: The social mask we wear in public
- **The Great Mother**: Nurturing or destructive maternal figure
- **The Wise Old Man**: Represents wisdom and meaning

### The Psyche Structure
Jung's model of the psyche:
- **Ego**: The center of consciousness
- **Personal Unconscious**: Personal repressed content
- **Collective Unconscious**: Universal inherited content

### Psychological Types
Jung's theory of personality types based on:
- **Attitudes**: Extraversion vs. Introversion
- **Functions**: Thinking, Feeling, Sensation, Intuition

### Individuation
The lifelong process of psychological development and self-realization:
- Integration of conscious and unconscious elements
- Development of the Self through confronting and integrating archetypal figures
- Movement toward wholeness and authenticity

## Therapeutic Techniques

### Active Imagination
A technique for engaging with unconscious material through imagination and dialogue with inner figures.

### Dream Analysis
Jung saw dreams as compensatory messages from the unconscious:
- Personal dreams reflect individual unconscious content
- Archetypal dreams contain universal symbols
- Dreams guide the process of individuation

### Word Association Tests
Used to uncover complexes (emotionally charged clusters of ideas) in the personal unconscious.

### Amplification
Expanding the meaning of dream symbols by connecting them to mythological, religious, and cultural parallels.

## Key Therapeutic Goals

- Facilitating the process of individuation
- Integrating the conscious and unconscious aspects of the personality
- Confronting and integrating shadow material
- Developing a more authentic, whole sense of self
- Understanding the symbolic meaning in life experiences
- Balancing opposing forces within the psyche
