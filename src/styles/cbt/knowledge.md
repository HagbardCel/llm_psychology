# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

## Core Concepts

### The Cognitive Model
CBT is based on the principle that thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected:
- **Cognitive**: Thoughts and beliefs about ourselves and the world
- **Affective**: Emotional responses
- **Behavioral**: Actions and behaviors

Changing one element can influence the others.

### Cognitive Distortions
Systematic patterns of thinking that are inaccurate or unhelpful:
- **All-or-Nothing Thinking**: Seeing things in black or white categories
- **Overgeneralization**: Viewing a single negative event as a never-ending pattern
- **Mental Filter**: Focusing only on negative details while ignoring positive aspects
- **Disqualifying the Positive**: Rejecting positive experiences by insisting they "don't count"
- **Jumping to Conclusions**: Making negative interpretations without supporting facts
- **Magnification/Minimization**: Exaggerating the importance of negative events
- **Emotional Reasoning**: Believing that feelings reflect reality
- **Should Statements**: Using critical words like "should," "must," or "ought"
- **Labeling**: Assigning global negative traits based on mistakes
- **Personalization**: Blaming yourself for events outside your control

### Core Beliefs
Deeply held assumptions about self, others, and the world that develop early in life and influence how we interpret experiences.

### Intermediate Beliefs
Rules, attitudes, and assumptions that are more situation-specific than core beliefs but still influence thinking and behavior.

### Automatic Thoughts
Spontaneous thoughts that occur in response to situations, often reflecting underlying beliefs and influencing emotions and behaviors.

## Therapeutic Techniques

### Cognitive Restructuring
Identifying and challenging distorted or unhelpful thinking patterns:
1. Identify the situation and emotional response
2. Record automatic thoughts
3. Examine evidence for and against the thoughts
4. Develop more balanced, realistic alternatives

### Behavioral Experiments
Testing the validity of beliefs through structured experiments and observing outcomes.

### Activity Scheduling
Planning and engaging in positive activities to improve mood and counteract avoidance behaviors.

### Behavioral Activation
Increasing engagement in rewarding activities to combat depression and low motivation.

### Exposure Therapy
Gradual, systematic exposure to feared situations or stimuli to reduce anxiety and avoidance.

### Homework Assignments
Practical exercises to practice skills learned in session and apply them to real-life situations.

### Thought Records
Structured worksheets to identify, examine, and restructure automatic thoughts.

## Key Therapeutic Goals

- Identify and modify dysfunctional thinking patterns
- Change maladaptive behaviors
- Develop practical coping strategies
- Solve current problems effectively
- Prevent relapse through skill-building
- Increase self-efficacy and confidence in managing difficulties
- Focus on the present and develop concrete solutions
